Howrah Bridge

The Howrah Bridge, officially named Rabindra Setu since 1965, is the iconic gateway to Kolkata and is widely considered the busiest cantilever bridge in the world. Spanning the Hooghly River to connect Kolkata and Howrah, it remains a marvel of engineering as it stands without any supporting pillars in the riverbed. As of May 1, 2026, the bridge is fully open 24 hours a day for regular traffic and pedestrians.

Victoria Memorial

The Victoria Memorial in Kolkata is a majestic white marble monument and museum dedicated to Queen Victoria. Built between 1906 and 1921, it is the largest monument in the world dedicated to a monarch and serves as a premier icon of the city. Its architecture is a blend of Indo-Saracenic revivalist styles, combining British, Mughal, and Venetian influences with marble from the same quarries as the Taj Mahal.

Princep Ghat

Prinsep Ghat is one of Kolkata's most iconic riverside destinations, famous for its historic white Palladian-style monument and panoramic views of the Hooghly River. Built in 1841 to commemorate Anglo-Indian scholar James Prinsep, the site features a grand neoclassical pavilion with Greek and Gothic architecture. Today, it serves as a tranquil retreat where locals and tourists gather for evening strolls, boat rides under the Vidyasagar Setu (Second Hooghly Bridge), and local street food.
